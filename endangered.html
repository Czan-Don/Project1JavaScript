Endangered Species in the Zoo

Bald Eagle

The bald eagle (Haliaeetus leucocephalus) is a bird of prey found in North America. It was once near extinction due to habitat destruction and pesticide use, but conservation efforts have helped its population recover.

One Horn Rhino

The One Horn Rhino (Pongo abelii) is one of the three orangutan species and is critically endangered. Habitat loss, illegal pet trade, and palm oil plantations pose significant threats to their survival.

Loggerhead Sea Turtle

The loggerhead sea turtle (Caretta caretta) is a marine reptile that is listed as vulnerable. Coastal development, pollution, and accidental capture in fishing gear are major threats to their populations.

Poison Dart Frog

The poison dart frog (Dendrobatidae) is a group of brightly colored frogs found in Central and South America. Habitat destruction and illegal pet trade are significant threats, and some species are critically endangered.

Black Puma

Black pumas are facing threats to their habitat and survival. Conservation initiatives are crucial to ensure their protection and the preservation of biodiversity.

If you have information about conservation organizations working to protect the black puma, consider supporting them to make a positive impact.

African Elephant

The African elephant is the world's largest land mammal and is threatened by habitat loss and poaching for ivory. Supporting conservation efforts is vital to their survival.

White Tiger

The White Tiger is critically endangered due to poaching for its horn. Conservation programs aim to protect these majestic creatures and their habitats.
